Our "Two-Lane" Diagnostic Approach

Every student comes in with different strengths. Before the bootcamp begins, each student will take a diagnostic exam (which we can combine with previous SAT scores, if available). This data allows us to recommend the best homework track for your student:

  • The Foundational Lane: Emphasizes building core accuracy and confidence. Homework heavily features foundational and medium-difficulty Khan Academy questions to ensure core mechanics are rock solid.
  • The Advanced Lane: Emphasizes speed and flexibility. Homework heavily features medium and advanced questions, focusing on hard Module 2 problems and time-saving shortcuts.

How it works in class: During our 75-minute live sessions, all students learn the same core concepts and strategies together. The differentiation happens entirely in the homework. This ensures that Foundational students are fully supported in keeping up with the exact same topics, while Advanced students are constantly challenged by higher-difficulty applications.

What We Cover

Over the 8 sessions, we aggressively target the highest-yield topics across the four main SAT Math domains:

  • Algebra: Linear equations (in 1 or 2 variables), Linear functions, System of 2 linear equations, Linear inequalities.
  • Geometry: Lines, Angles, Triangles (isosceles, right, similar), Circles, Trigonometry.
  • Problem-Solving and Data Analysis: Ratios/rates/proportions, Percentages, Data distributions, Probability.
  • Advanced Math: Nonlinear equations (quadratics, polynomials), Equivalent expressions, Exponential functions.

The Score-Growth Methodology

We don't just review math; we teach students how to take the test. Our repeatable weekly structure is designed to convert knowledge into points:

  1. Teach Strategy: Introduce the concept and the SAT-specific translation.
  2. Desmos Mastery: Demonstrate when the digital calculator is faster than standard algebra.
  3. Timed Micro-Sprints: Students solve problems in short, timed bursts with live review to build test-day pacing.
  4. Error Logging: Students track their mistakes by type (e.g., Concept Error vs. Setup Error vs. Desmos Misuse) to fix the root cause of lost points.
  5. Personalized Homework: Students complete targeted practice based on their specific Lane and Error Log.
