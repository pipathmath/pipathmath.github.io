Our "Two-Lane" Diagnostic Approach

Every student comes in with different strengths. Before the bootcamp begins, each student will take a diagnostic exam (or provide previous SAT scores, if available). This data allows us to recommend the best homework track for your student:

  • The Foundational Lane: Emphasizes building core accuracy and confidence. Homework heavily features foundational and medium-difficulty questions, with fewer high-difficulty questions.
  • The Advanced Lane: Emphasizes problem-solving speed and advanced techniques for higher-difficulty questions. Homework focuses on hard Module 2 problems and time-saving shortcuts.

How it works in class: During our 75-minute live sessions, all students learn the same core concepts and strategies together. The differentiation happens entirely in the homework. This ensures that Foundational students are fully supported in keeping up with the exact same topics, while Advanced students are constantly challenged by higher-difficulty applications through homework.

What We Cover

Over our 8 sessions, we aggressively target the highest-yield topics across the four main SAT Math domains. Instead of randomly reviewing topics, we follow a progressive, week-by-week syllabus designed for maximum score growth:

  • Week 1: Algebra Foundation
    Creating linear equations, interpreting slope and intercepts, tackling systems of equations, and mastering the SAT strategy of "translating words into mathematical structure."
  • Week 2: Advanced Math
    Standard, factored, and vertex forms of quadratics, polynomials, exponential growth, radicals, and recognizing equivalent forms to bypass heavy calculations.
  • Week 3: Problem-Solving and Data Analysis
    Ratios, consecutive percent changes, multi-step word problems, standard deviation intuition, scatterplots, and avoiding conditional probability traps.
  • Week 4: Geometry, Trigonometry & Final Strategy
    Similar and special right triangles, circle equations, and SOH-CAH-TOA. We conclude with a full mixed-domain simulation and a final pacing strategy for test day.

The Score-Growth Methodology

We don't just review math; we teach students how to take the test. Our repeatable weekly structure is designed to convert knowledge into points:

  1. Teaching Strategy: Introduce the concepts alongside the SAT-specific strategies.
  2. Desmos Mastery: Demonstrate when the digital calculator is faster than standard algebra.
  3. Timed Problem-Solving Sprints: Students solve problems in short, timed bursts with live review to build test-day pacing.
  4. Personalized Homework: Students complete targeted practice based on their specific struggle areas.
